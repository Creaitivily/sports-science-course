# The 4-Pillar Operational Structure

Person B views the business through four distinct departments.

---

## 1. Strategy Department (The Brain)

Before any camera rolls, Person B must define the roadmap.

### Responsibilities
| Task | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Market Research** | Analyzing competitors and trending formats |
| **Content Pillars** | Defining the 3-4 core topics the brand is known for |
| **Content Calendar** | Planning videos 2–4 weeks in advance |
| **Scripting/Briefing** | Creating the "Blueprints" with hooks, talking points, and CTAs |

---

## 2. Systems & Automation Department (The Engine)

Leverage automation to scale output without scaling hours.

### Key Systems

| System | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Central OS** | Notion/ClickUp/Trello as "Single Source of Truth" |
| **Capture Systems** | Easy way for Person A to dump ideas (voice notes → content bank) |
| **Posting Automation** | Buffer/Metricool/Make.com for scheduled posting |
| **Asset Management** | Automated folder structures with auto-sorting |

---

## 3. Production Management Department (The Factory)

Person B acts as the "Director" to Person A's "Actor."

### Production Deliverables

| Deliverable | Contents |
|-------------|----------|
| **The Shoot Brief** | Script, B-roll list, outfit requirements, tonality notes |
| **Environment Setup** | Standardized lighting/audio for near-zero setup time |
| **File Transfer Protocol** | System for footage → cloud immediately after shooting |

---

## 4. Growth & Analytics Department (The Scoreboard)

Close the loop by analyzing what worked.

### Focus Areas

| Area | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Data Analysis** | Review retention charts, CTR, conversion metrics |
| **Feedback Loops** | Weekly meeting: "Do more of X, stop doing Y" |
| **Community Management** | Systems for comment replies and lead engagement |
