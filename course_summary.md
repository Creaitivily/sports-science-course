## CORE CONTENT

### 1. Foundations of Training Theory

#### 1.1 Biological Principles
| Principle | Definition | Timeframe Relevance |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Progressive Overload** | Systematic stimulus increase | Monthly/Weekly progression schemes |
| **Specificity** | Adaptations match stimulus type | Annual general→specific transition |
| **Reversibility** | Detraining without stimulus | Weekly session frequency, transition periods |
| **Individualization** | Individual response variation | Daily readiness monitoring |
| **Variability** | Stimulus alternation | Weekly load undulation |

#### 1.2 Physiological Adaptations
- **Neuromuscular:** Motor unit recruitment, neural synchronization, intermuscular coordination
- **Metabolic:** Mitochondrial density, oxidative capacity, substrate economy
- **Cardiovascular:** Cardiac hypertrophy, stroke volume, capillarization
- **Structural:** Muscle hypertrophy, tendon remodeling, bone density

---

### 2. Periodization Models

#### 2.1 Classical Model (Matveyev/Bompa)
Hierarchical temporal organization: Macrocycle → Mesocycle → Microcycle → Session
- Sequential development of capacities
- Volume-to-intensity inverse relationship
- Single or double periodization for 1-2 peaks

#### 2.2 Block Periodization (Issurin)
Concentrated load blocks focusing on 2-3 capacities:
- Exploitation of residual training effects
- Multiple peak capability
- Flexible calendar adaptation

#### 2.3 Tactical Periodization
Game-model centered approach for team sports:
- Morphocycle as weekly training unit
- Specificity principle dominance
- Horizontal alternation of intensity/complexity

---

### 3. Training Load Monitoring

#### 3.1 External Load (GPS Metrics)
| Metric | What It Measures | When to Monitor |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| Total Distance | Volume | Weekly totals, session comparison |
| High-Speed Running | Intensity indicator | Match vs. training comparison |
| Player Load | Multi-directional stress | Daily accumulation |
| Sprint Distance | Maximal efforts | Weekly exposure tracking |
| Accelerations/Decelerations | High-intensity actions | Match demand replication |

#### 3.2 Internal Load (Physiological Response)
- Heart rate zones and TRIMP
- Session-RPE (sRPE)
- Wellness questionnaires
- HRV readiness monitoring

#### 3.3 Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio (ACWR)
Weekly monitoring of recent load (acute, ~7 days) relative to longer-term load (chronic, ~28 days) to optimize injury risk management.

---

### 4. Sleep, Recovery, and Performance

**Scientific Note:** Research by this course's coordinator demonstrated that metabolic changes explain how training can mitigate strength reduction caused by sleep restriction (Physiology & Behavior, 2025).

#### 4.1 Sleep Impact by Timeframe
| Timeframe | Sleep Consideration |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Annual | Sleep periodization aligned with training phases |
| Monthly | Sleep quality tracking for adaptation verification |
| Weekly | Strategic napping, travel sleep management |
| Daily | 7-9 hour target, pre-sleep routine, sleep hygiene |
