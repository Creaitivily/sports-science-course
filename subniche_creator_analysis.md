# Course Subniches and Top Creator Analysis

## Purpose
This document maps the Sports Science course content to social media subniches and identifies top content creators in each area to inform audience building strategy.

---

| Subniche | Course Discipline | Content Focus | Audience Demand |

| 4 | **Applied Biomechanics** | Therapist-focused, not coach-focused | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| 5 | **Mental Skills for Coaches** | Athlete-focused, not coach tools | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ |
| 6 | **Evidence-Based Recovery** | Anecdotal content dominates | ⭐⭐⭐ |

### Content Format Recommendations

| Platform | Best Content Type | Subniches to Prioritize |
|----------|------------------|------------------------|
| **YouTube** | Long-form education, demonstrations | Periodization, Biomechanics, Sleep |
| **Instagram** | Carousels, Reels, quick tips | All — gateway content |
| **TikTok** | Myth-busting, quick protocols | Nutrition, Mobility, Psychology |
| **Podcast** | Deep-dives, interviews | Sleep Science, Mental Performance |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional education | S&C coaches, Sport science careers |

### Competitive Positioning

**Unique Angles from Course Content:**
1. **Instructor's Research** — Sleep-training interactions (Daubian-Nosé, 2025)
2. **Integrated Approach** — Cross-discipline connections (training × sleep × psychology)
3. **Coach-Focused** — Most content targets athletes; coach education is under-served
4. **Evidence-Based + Practical** — Bridge between academic and gym-floor application
5. **Portuguese/Brazilian Market** — Potential for Portuguese-language content
